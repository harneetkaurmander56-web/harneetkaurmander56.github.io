Essay on Shri Guru Nanak Dev ji in 200 words

Guru Nanak Dev ji,the founder of Sikhism was the Great leader and
teacher who spread the message of truth, peace and equality. His teachings are
simple yet powerful and remain highly relevant, especially for students in todays
fast-moving world.
He gave three main principles: “Naam Japo” (remember God), “Kirat Karo” (earn through honest work)
and "vand chako" (share with others). These teachings
guide students in both their academic and personal lives. “Naam Japo” helps
students stay calm, focused, and mentally strong during studies and exams. It
encourages a positive mindset and reduces stress.
“Kirat Karo” teaches the importance of honesty and hard work.
Students should complete their work sincerely and avoid cheating or taking shortcuts.
True success comes from dedication and effort. “Vand Chhako” reminds students to be
kind and helpful by sharing knowledge, helping classmates, and supporting
those who are struggling.
Guru Nanak Dev Ji also strongly believed in equality. He taught that all people
are equal regardless of caste, religion, or status. This helps students develop
respect, unity, and friendship with others.
By following his teachings, students can grow into responsible, honest, and kind
individuals. These values not only help in achieving success in studies but also in
becoming better human beings and contributing positively to society.
